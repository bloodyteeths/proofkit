üìã Detailed validation criteria
  • Temperature control: Chamber temperature must reach and maintain 50-60¬∞C throughout exposure phase. Higher temperatures improve gas penetration and reduce cycle time.
  • Humidity validation: Relative humidity 45-85% required for proper microbial kill. Too low reduces efficacy; too high causes condensation problems.
  • Gas concentration: EtO levels monitored continuously. Minimum 600 mg/L typical for medical devices, with leakage compensation calculated automatically.
  • Exposure time calculation: Begins when all parameters (temperature, humidity, gas concentration) simultaneously reach target values and continues until aeration phase.
  • Pressure monitoring: Sub-atmospheric pressure maintenance validated to prevent gas leaks and ensure uniform distribution throughout load.
  • Aeration validation: Post-exposure air changes and residual EtO levels tracked to ensure safe handling of sterilized items.

üéØ Why is EtO sterilization used instead of steam autoclaving?

EtO sterilization operates at low temperatures (50-60¬∞C) making it ideal for heat-sensitive materials like plastics, electronics, and complex medical devices. It penetrates packaging and reaches all surfaces, including lumens and crevices that steam cannot access effectively.
